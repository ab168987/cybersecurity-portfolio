# Phishing Email Analysis

## Objective
The goal of this project was to analyze a suspicious email and identify common phishing techniques used to deceive users and steal sensitive information.

## Tools Used
- TryHackMe lab environment
- Email analysis techniques (manual inspection)

## Methodology
- Reviewed the sender’s email address for inconsistencies
- Analyzed the email content for urgency and manipulation tactics
- Inspected embedded links for suspicious or mismatched URLs
- Identified indicators of spoofing and social engineering

## Key Findings
- The email used urgent language to pressure the recipient
- The sender address appeared legitimate but contained subtle misspellings
- The embedded link redirected to a non-official domain
- The message attempted to trick the user into providing sensitive information

## Security Implications
- Users may unknowingly provide credentials to attackers
- Phishing attacks remain one of the most common entry points for breaches
- Awareness and verification are critical in preventing compromise

## What I Learned
- How to identify phishing indicators
- Common social engineering tactics used in attacks
- The importance of verifying email sources and links before interacting
